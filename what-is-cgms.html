What is continuous glucose monitoring system(CGMS)?

A Continuous Glucose Monitoring System (CGMS) is a medical device that tracks glucose levels in real time through a small wearable sensor placed under the skin. Unlike traditional fingerprick testing, which provides a single glucose reading at one moment, CGMS offers continuous data, showing trends, patterns, and alerts for high or low glucose levels —helping healthcare providers and patients make more informed, proactive treatment decisions.

How does CGMS work?

1

Once the CGM is applied to your arm, the sensor begins working beneath the skin.

2

The sensor contains biomaterials that react with glucose in the interstitial fluid. These reactions generate biochemical signals, which are detected and converted into glucose values every few minutes.

3

The transmitter then sends this data to your phone app or receiver.

4

Allowing you to view glucose trends, receive high and low alerts, and monitor changes throughout the day.

Note: Since glucose naturally moves from the bloodstream into the interstitial fluid, interstitial glucose readings closely reflect blood glucose levels.

Are CGMS worth it for healthcare providers?

Adding CGM to your healthcare portfolio creates clinical and commercial value across multiple dimensions.

Retain market share

As CGM adoption grows, offering CGM solutions helps your business stay competitive in an evolving diabetes care market and meet changing customer expectations.

Expand product category

Add CGM to your existing diabetes, diagnostic, or chronic care portfolio, creating extra value for clients and new opportunities for growth.

Grow revenue

CGM systems rely on disposable sensors that are typically replaced every 14 days, allowing you to benefit from continuous repeat orders rather than one-time purchases.

Improve patient treatment

CGM gives you trend-based glucose insights instead of isolated measurements, helping doctors and healthcare teams make informed treatment decisions.

Improve clinical efficiency

You can reduce reliance on scattered glucose checks and integrate continuous glucose data into digital health workflows, improving patient monitoring beyond in-person visits.

Support preventive monitoring

CGM allows care teams to monitor patients' glucose changes continuously, helping identify abnormal patterns before they become more serious.
