What is CGM SKD?

CGM SKD (Semi-Knocked-Down) is a manufacturing model beneficial for healthcare businesses looking to launch a CGM brand fast while meeting local assembly or manufacturing requirements.

Instead of importing fully finished products, you receive partially assembled kits from us, including the sensor core, transmitter, and applicator. Your team completes final assembly, packaging, and labeling locally for sale. We also provide technical support and guidance for manufacturing setup and regulatory preparation such as CE registration.

Who is CGM SKD for?

Four types of healthcare businesses that benefit most from the semi-knocked-down CGM manufacturing model.

Government tender bidders

Deeply familiar with local regulations but blocked by strict local content rules for finished imports? Under SKD partnership model, we help you fulfill all local assembly requirements, giving you an immediate compliant path to qualify for and win public healthcare tenders.

Healthcare brands

Equipped with strong brand power but trapped by the massive R&D costs of developing sensor technology from scratch? Our SKD model offers a ready-to-assemble shortcut to launch your own CGM brand while retaining full ownership of the final product.

Medical manufacturers

Possessing advanced assembly lines or clean rooms but wanting to utilize this existing infrastructure to its fullest potential? Add CGM to your current pipeline through our SKD model and maximize your manufacturing advantages while lowering local operational and logistics costs.

Established distributors

Armed with a mature sales network but tired of reselling third-party brands and fighting price wars that erode your margins? Switching to our SKD model empowers you to launch your own branding, cutting out upstream markups to regain absolute pricing control.

We supply you with proprietary high-precision sensor chips, wireless transmitter PCBA, housings, adhesive patches and battery units pre-assembled and ready for your final integration.

Documentation support

You receive supporting documentation that simplify local regulatory registration, such as electronic performance verification reports, biocompatibility assessment, ISO13485, clinical study data reports, and IEC standard test reports, etc.

Software ecosystem access

A dedicated patient or doctor app is available via API or SDK connection that supports glucose monitoring and reporting. You can also integrate it into your digital healthcare platform.

Quality control framework

You receive inspection standards, verification procedures, and automated testing equipment to support stable assembly quality. These help your team validate component performance, reduce assembly variation, and maintain reliable quality before final product release.

Stable CGM components supply

We possess large-scale manufacturing capacity, able to produce millions of CGM units annually. As your business grows, we scale with you. No unexpected shortages. No production delays on your end.

Assembly and calibration guidelines

You receive comprehensive kit specifications, component lists, production line equipment recommendations, and standardized operating procedures (SOPs) to support efficient assembly setup and consistent calibration workflows.

Frequently asked questions

Assembly is extremely straightforward. Every component features snap-fit mechanisms, and you will hear an audible click when each part is correctly in place. Also, we provide detailed instructions, cleanroom guidelines, quality assurance procedures, and training to ensure smooth assembly and reliable device performance.

OEM means we manufacture fully finished, ready-to-use CGM devices for you to brand and sell directly. Regulatory certifications such as CE or FDA remain under our responsibility, and you operate under a distributor/importer model.

With CGM SKD, we send you separate components including sensor, transmitter, adhesive patch, and applicator. You assemble them locally, which reduces shipping volume and lowers import tariffs since SKD often qualifies for lower duties. However, you must obtain your own local manufacturing or assembly license, complete final quality checks, and take full regulatory responsibility for the finished CGM products in your market.

OEM is simpler but relatively more costly. SKD is more flexible, and can often reduce import duties, or meet "Made in Country" requirements but adds regulatory and quality control duties.
