    The Government Tender Opportunity

    Government healthcare tenders represent a significant opportunity for medical device companies. Many countries, particularly in Southeast Asia, the Middle East, Africa, and Latin America, are investing heavily in diabetes care infrastructure, including CGM systems.

    However, winning these tenders requires meeting specific qualification criteria, including local content requirements, technology transfer commitments, and after-sales service capabilities.

    The SKD Advantage

    The Semi-Knocked Down (SKD) manufacturing model offers a unique competitive edge in government tender bidding.

    Local Value-Add Requirements

    Many government tenders require a minimum percentage of local value-add. By importing CGM components through the SKD model and performing final assembly locally, you can meet these requirements without building a full manufacturing facility from scratch.

    Reduced Import Duties

    Importing components rather than finished products typically qualifies for lower tariff rates. This cost advantage can be reflected in more competitive tender pricing, improving your bid position.

    Local Job Creation

    Government tender evaluation criteria often include local economic impact. SKD assembly operations create local jobs in manufacturing, quality control, and logistics, strengthening your tender application.

    Regulatory Pathway Support

    EverChek provides comprehensive technical documentation and regulatory support for SKD partners, including quality management system documentation, test reports, and compliance materials that simplify local regulatory approval.

    Case Study: SKD Success

    A healthcare company in Southeast Asia partnered with EverChek through the SKD model to bid on a national diabetes management program. By establishing a local assembly line and demonstrating technology transfer commitment, they secured a three-year government contract worth over $5 million.

    Getting Started

    Entering government tender markets with SKD is a structured process: assess target market tender requirements, develop local assembly infrastructure, establish quality management system, prepare tender documentation with manufacturer support, and submit competitive bid with SKD cost advantage.
